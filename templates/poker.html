Poker Hands

By combining the cards on the table, and in your hand, what is the best 5 card hand you can create?

Here are the possible hands, ranked from best to worst:

  1. Royal Flush


    Highest possible consecutive cards, in-suit.

  2. Straight Flush


    Five consecutive cards, in-suit.

  3. Four of a Kind


    All four of a single rank card, one of each suit
    (1 unused card).

  4. Full House


    Any Pair, and any Three-of-a-kind, together.

  5. Flush


    Five cards non-consecutive cards of the same suit.
    (The highest ranked card defines the hand, i.e. 'King flush').

  6. Straight


    Five consecutive cards, not in-suit.

  7. Three of a Kind


    Three of the same ranked card
    (2 unused cards).

  8. Two Pair


    Two of the same ranked card, and two of another.
    (1 unused card).

  9. Pair


    Two of the same ranked card
    (3 unused cards).

  10. High Card


    Your one highest ranked card
    (4 unused cards).
    This is the worst possible hand.

The player who has the highest ranking hand wins.
If hand ranks match, the hand with the highest card values wins.
If there is still a tie, the highest ranked unused cards (if any) decide the winner.
